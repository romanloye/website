Projects

Here is a sample of projects I’ve worked on recently, from programming at a public library to my personal jewelry business.

2022

PIÑATA PARTY

In this program for Hispanic Heritage Month, I developed and hosted a bilingual workshop to teach participants how to design and build a 2-dimensional piñata. Participants of all ages left the workshop with a fully made piñata and the knowledge to repeat the craft at home.

SKETCH-A-BOOK

I helped design a beginner-friendly, age-inclusive, and bilingual arts program aimed to reuse books withdrawn from our libraries’ circulation as sketchbooks. Every week we met at the library for an hour and a half to draw, paint and sketch in our books based on a different theme or technique, with the ultimate goal of showing our work at a library exhibition.

MEDIA SCIENCE ILLUSTRATION

Collaborated with scientists from the University of Washington, to develop illustrations of now extinct marine species for communication of research findings. This work was featured in Forbes etc.

BOOK DISPLAYS

I really enjoy creating book displays to showcase culturally inclusive selections of themes relevant to a community. Recently, I created a popular Astrology display with materials for beginner and intermediate astrology enthusiasts, and another one showcasing the best fiction releases of 2022.

SCIENTIST WEBSITE

I designed and built a portfolio website with an early-Internet aesthetic for an environmental scientist at Princeton University.

2021

KIOSKO

I started a jewelry project where I designed and made playful, 0-plastic, one-of-a-kind costume jewelry incorporating beading techniques such as looming, brick stitch, and daisy chain, among others. All marketing materials and images were also made by me.
